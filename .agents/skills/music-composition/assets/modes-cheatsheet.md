# Modes Cheatsheet

Quick reference for the seven diatonic modes, plus important non-diatonic modal scales. For full discussion, see `harmony/modal-harmony.md` and `fundamentals/pitch-intervals-scales.md`.

## The seven diatonic modes

The diatonic modes can be understood in two complementary ways:

1. **Relative mode**: start on a different scale degree of one parent major scale.
2. **Parallel mode**: keep the same tonic and alter scale degrees relative to major/minor.

### Relative modes in C major

| Mode | Starts on in C major | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| **Ionian** | C (^1) | C D E F G A B |
| **Dorian** | D (^2) | D E F G A B C |
| **Phrygian** | E (^3) | E F G A B C D |
| **Lydian** | F (^4) | F G A B C D E |
| **Mixolydian** | G (^5) | G A B C D E F |
| **Aeolian** | A (^6) | A B C D E F G |
| **Locrian** | B (^7) | B C D E F G A |

### Parallel modes on C

| Mode | Notes (C tonic) | Formula | Character |
|------|-----------------|---------|-----------|
| **Ionian** | C D E F G A B | `W W H W W W H` | Major; bright; the default |
| **Dorian** | C D E♭ F G A B♭ | `W H W W W H W` | Minor with raised ^6; cool, modal |
| **Phrygian** | C D♭ E♭ F G A♭ B♭ | `H W W W H W W` | Minor with ♭2; severe, flamenco-adjacent or metal color when used idiomatically |
| **Lydian** | C D E F♯ G A B | `W W W H W W H` | Major with ♯4; floating, dreamy |
| **Mixolydian** | C D E F G A B♭ | `W W H W W H W` | Major with ♭7; bluesy, rock |
| **Aeolian** | C D E♭ F G A♭ B♭ | `W H W W H W W` | Natural minor; somber |
| **Locrian** | C D♭ E♭ F G♭ A♭ B♭ | `H W W H W W W` | Diminished tonic; rare; theoretical |

(`W` = whole step, `H` = half step.)

## Quick mode identification

To name a mode, identify (a) the tonic and (b) which note is altered relative to major or minor:

### Brighter than major (Lydian)

- Major scale with ♯4 (♯11) → Lydian
- The "happy floating" sound

### Major-like (Ionian, Mixolydian)

- Pure major → Ionian
- Major with ♭7 → Mixolydian

### Minor-like (Aeolian, Dorian)

- Pure natural minor → Aeolian
- Natural minor with ♮6 (raised 6th) → Dorian

### Darker than minor (Phrygian, Locrian)

- Natural minor with ♭2 → Phrygian
- Natural minor with ♭2 and ♭5 → Locrian

## Characteristic chords (the "color" chords) per mode

The chord that most distinguishes each mode from major/minor:

| Mode | Characteristic chord | Notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| Ionian | I | The plain major; no special color |
| Dorian | IV (major IV from minor mode!) | The major IV is the Dorian giveaway |
| Phrygian | ♭II | The half-step-up chord — Phrygian's calling card |
| Lydian | II | The major II (a whole step up from tonic, major) |
| Mixolydian | ♭VII | The whole-step-down major chord |
| Aeolian | iv (minor iv) and ♭VI, ♭VII | Standard natural-minor chords |
| Locrian | i° (diminished tonic) | The diminished tonic — instability built in |

## Common modal progressions

### Dorian

| Progression | Example in D Dorian |
|-------------|---------------------|
| i-IV | Dm - G |
| i-IV-vii-i | Dm - G - C - Dm |
| i-iv-IV-i (mixed) | Dm - Gm - G - Dm |

### Phrygian

| Progression | Example in E Phrygian |
|-------------|----------------------|
| i-♭II | Em - F |
| i-♭II-i | Em - F - Em |
| ♭II-♭VII-i | F - D - Em |

### Lydian

| Progression | Example in F Lydian |
|-------------|--------------------|
| I-II | F - G |
| I-II-vii-I | F - G - Em - F |
| I-II-iii-I | F - G - Am - F |

### Mixolydian

| Progression | Example in G Mixolydian |
|-------------|------------------------|
| I-♭VII | G - F |
| I-♭VII-IV-I | G - F - C - G |
| I-V-IV-I | G - D - C - G (with ♭VII implied) |

### Aeolian (natural minor)

| Progression | Example in A Aeolian |
|-------------|---------------------|
| i-VII-VI-VII | Am - G - F - G |
| i-iv-VII | Am - Dm - G |
| i-VI-III-VII | Am - F - C - G |

## Modes of melodic minor (jazz minor)

Built from the ascending melodic minor scale (= natural minor with ♮6 and ♮7, both raised).

| Mode | Parent-scale degree | Notes (parallel C tonic) | Use over |
|------|------------------------------|-----------------|----------|
| Melodic minor | ^1 | C D E♭ F G A B | Cm(maj7); tonic minor in jazz |
| Dorian ♭2 | ^2 | C D♭ E♭ F G A B♭ | iim7♭5 contexts |
| Lydian augmented | ^3 | C D E F♯ G♯ A B | Cmaj7♯5; floating, ambiguous |
| Lydian dominant | ^4 | C D E F♯ G A B♭ | C7♯11 (non-resolving dominant) |
| Mixolydian ♭6 | ^5 | C D E F G A♭ B♭ | C7♭13 |
| Locrian ♮2 | ^6 | C D E♭ F G♭ A♭ B♭ | iiø7 contexts |
| **Altered (super Locrian)** | ^7 | C D♭ D♯ E G♭/F♯ A♭ B♭ | **C7alt** (resolving dominants; functional spelling) |

The **altered scale** is the workhorse of modern jazz — used over `V7alt` resolving to `i`. In theoretical melodic-minor mode spelling, some notes may be written as E♭/F♭; in functional dominant spelling, label them as ♭9, ♯9, 3, ♭5/♯11, ♭13, and ♭7 so the chord function stays clear.

## Modes of harmonic minor

Built from the harmonic minor scale (natural minor with raised ^7 only).

| Mode | Built on | Use |
|------|----------|-----|
| Harmonic minor | ^1 | Tonic in some classical and jazz minor contexts |
| Locrian ♮6 | ^2 | iiø7 with raised 6 (less common) |
| Ionian ♯5 | ^3 | Major with augmented 5 (Cmaj7♯5 in some contexts) |
| Dorian ♯4 | ^4 | Folk and Eastern European |
| **Phrygian dominant** | ^5 | **V7♭9 resolving to minor**; flamenco, Klezmer, metal |
| Lydian ♯2 | ^6 | Rare |
| Super-locrian ♭♭7 | ^7 | Very rare |

The **Phrygian dominant scale** (e.g., G Phrygian dominant: G A♭ B C D E♭ F) is a common Western shorthand for dominant-minor tension and is often heard in flamenco-adjacent, klezmer-adjacent, metal, and some maqam-/makam-adjacent fusion contexts. The augmented 2nd between ♭2 and 3 is its signature, but tradition-specific practice requires more than choosing this scale.

## Pentatonic and blues

### Major pentatonic

C D E G A — the first five notes of the major scale, omitting ^4 and ^7. Avoids half-steps. Folk, country, gospel.

### Minor pentatonic

C E♭ F G B♭ — minor scale with ^2 and ^6 omitted. Blues, rock.

### Blues scale

Minor pentatonic + ♭5: C E♭ F G♭ G B♭. The ♭5 is a "blue note" — a passing chromaticism.

### Major blues scale

Major pentatonic + ♭3: C D E♭ E G A. Used over major-mode blues, rockabilly, country.

## Symmetric scales

These have no clear tonic — the interval pattern repeats within the octave.

| Scale | Pattern | Notes (C) | Use |
|-------|---------|-----------|-----|
| Whole tone | `W W W W W W` (6 notes) | C D E F♯ G♯ B♭ | Over 7♯5 / whole-tone dominant colors; B♭ is the dominant spelling of the enharmonic A♯ |
| Diminished (whole-half) | `W H W H W H W H` (8 notes) | C D E♭ F G♭ A♭ A B | Over diminished chords |
| Diminished (half-whole) | `H W H W H W H W` (8 notes) | C D♭ D♯ E F♯ G A B♭ | Over dominant ♭9 chords; D♯ is ♯9 over C7 |
| Augmented | `A2 H A2 H A2 H` (6 notes) | C D♯ E G A♭ B | Symmetric augmented sound (A2 = augmented 2nd, enharmonic to m3) |

## Other useful scales

| Scale | Notes (C) | Origin/character |
|-------|-----------|------------------|
| Hungarian minor | C D E♭ F♯ G A♭ B | Hungarian folk, Romani |
| Byzantine / double harmonic | C D♭ E F G A♭ B | Greek, Middle Eastern; two augmented 2nds |
| Bebop major | C D E F G A♭ A B | Major + ♯5 added passing |
| Bebop dominant | C D E F G A B♭ B | Mixolydian + ♮7 added passing |
| Bebop dorian | C D E♭ E F G A B♭ | Dorian + ♮3 added passing |
| Hirajōshi (Japanese) | C D E♭ G A♭ | Japanese pentatonic; haunting |

## Mode-to-mode comparisons (which is brighter?)

Bright to dark, with C tonic:

```
Lydian (C D E F♯ G A B) — brightest
Ionian (C D E F G A B)
Mixolydian (C D E F G A B♭)
Dorian (C D E♭ F G A B♭)
Aeolian (C D E♭ F G A♭ B♭)
Phrygian (C D♭ E♭ F G A♭ B♭)
Locrian (C D♭ E♭ F G♭ A♭ B♭) — darkest
```

This ordering corresponds to the modes' positions on the cycle of fifths — Lydian has the most sharps relative to the parallel major; Locrian has the most flats.

## Quick decision matrix

| Want | Try |
|------|-----|
| Bright, modal-major | Lydian (with ♯4) or Ionian |
| Major-with-bluesy-edge | Mixolydian (with ♭7) |
| Cool, modal-minor | Dorian (with ♮6) |
| Standard minor | Aeolian (natural minor) |
| Dark, severe, half-step-above-tonic color | Phrygian (with ♭2) |
| Flamenco-adjacent / klezmer-adjacent dominant-minor color | Phrygian dominant, with tradition-specific caution |
| Jazz tonic minor | Melodic minor |
| Jazz altered dominant | Altered scale (super-Locrian) |
| Jazz non-resolving dominant | Lydian dominant |
| Floating, ambiguous | Whole tone |
| Diminished color | Either diminished scale (whole-half or half-whole) |
| Folk / pentatonic safety | Major or minor pentatonic |
| Blues | Blues scale or minor pentatonic + ♭5 |
