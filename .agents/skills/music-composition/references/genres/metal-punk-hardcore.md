# Metal, Punk, and Hardcore

> **Snapshot note:** Last content review snapshot: 2026-04-27. Use this file for durable composition conventions, but verify current subscene terms, production norms, artist references, and platform trends with active web research. See `../research/web-music-trend-research.md` and `../research/reference-track-digging.md`.

This file covers composition-facing conventions in punk, hardcore, metal, pop-punk, metalcore, post-hardcore, thrash-adjacent writing, doom/sludge-adjacent writing, and heavy guitar music generally. It is **not** a complete guide to every metal or punk subgenre.

## When to consult this file

- The user wants punk, hardcore, metal, metalcore, pop-punk, post-hardcore, emo-adjacent, thrash, doom, sludge, or heavy guitar writing
- The user asks for riffs, breakdowns, chugs, tremolo picking, blast-like energy, mosh sections, or aggressive band texture
- The user wants to make a pop/rock song heavier
- The user wants to write guitar/bass/drum parts that feel playable rather than generic MIDI blocks
- The user wants intense energy without simply making everything louder

## Core idea

In metal, punk, and hardcore, **riff, rhythm, attack, and group energy** are often more structurally important than chord progressions.

A useful diagnostic:

```text
What is the physical action?
- head-nod / stomp
- sprint / pogo
- mosh / push-pull
- slow crush
- anthem singalong
- technical precision
```

Then write the rhythm, register, articulation, and form around that action.

## Substyle map

| Frame | Composition priorities | Common signs |
|---|---|---|
| Punk rock | speed, directness, short forms, shoutable hooks | downstrokes, power chords, fast backbeat |
| Pop-punk | punk energy + pop chorus | I-V-vi-IV, octave guitars, gang vocals |
| Hardcore punk | intensity, stops, short cells, group chants | fast tempos, breakdowns, shouted vocals, DIY directness |
| Post-hardcore / emo-adjacent | tension-release, dynamic contrast, melodic hooks | angular riffs, wide choruses, screamed/sung contrast |
| Heavy metal | riff architecture, distorted guitars, powerful vocals | power chords, modal minor, gallops, solos |
| Thrash-adjacent | speed, tight picking, palm-muted precision | E-string riffs, fast downpicking/alternate picking |
| Doom/sludge-adjacent | slow tempo, weight, repetition | low tuning, long sustain, chromatic descent |
| Metalcore | riff + breakdown + hook architecture | chugs, syncopation, half-time drops, clean chorus |
| Djent/prog-metal-adjacent | meter games, low strings, syncopated cells | odd groupings, tight gates, extended-range guitar |

## Harmony and pitch language

Heavy music often uses pitch as **riff contour** rather than functional harmony.

### Common pitch centers and modes

| Sound | Material |
|---|---|
| Straight punk/rock | major/minor pentatonic, I-IV-V, I-♭VII-IV |
| Dark metal | Aeolian, Phrygian, harmonic minor colors |
| Aggressive low riff | tonic pedal + ♭2/♭5/♭6 chromatic cells |
| Epic / power metal | natural minor, harmonic minor, modal mixture, sequence |
| Doom/sludge | chromatic descent, tritone, low pedal, slow modal loops |
| Pop-punk chorus | major key or relative minor with clear tonal pull |

### Progression families

| Use | Progression | Example in E |
|---|---|---|
| Punk anthem | I-♭VII-IV-I | E-D-A-E |
| Pop-punk chorus | I-V-vi-IV | E-B-C♯m-A |
| Dark minor | i-♭VI-♭VII-i | Em-C-D-Em |
| Phrygian bite | i-♭II-i | Em-F-Em |
| Metal sequence | i-♭VII-♭VI-V | Em-D-C-B7 |
| Doom loop | i-♭V-iv-i | Em-B♭-Am-Em |
| Hardcore chant | i / ♭VII / i stops | E5 / D5 / E5 hits |

Use full extended chords sparingly unless the substyle calls for prog, jazz-metal, post-rock, or emo color.

## Riff writing

A riff is not just a chord progression. It is a repeated physical pattern with identity.

### Riff variables

| Variable | Choices |
|---|---|
| Register | low-string pedal, mid-neck power chords, octave line, high tremolo line |
| Articulation | palm-muted, open, staccato, slide, bend, pinch harmonic, tremolo |
| Rhythm | straight 8ths, gallop, syncopated chug, triplet feel, odd grouping |
| Pitch | pedal + neighbor, chromatic cell, modal fragment, power-chord movement |
| Density | full band unison, guitar-only, bass counter-riff, drum-accented hits |

### Basic riff recipes

#### Punk downstroke riff

```text
Tempo: 176 BPM
Guitar: all downstrokes, power chords
| E5 E5 E5 E5 | A5 A5 B5 B5 |
Vocal: shoutable 3-5 note hook, little melisma.
```

#### Metal pedal riff

```text
Center: E
Pattern: E pedal on 8ths, accents on F and G
E E F E | E G F E |
Effect: Phrygian bite without needing complex harmony.
```

#### Metalcore breakdown seed

```text
Tempo: 150 BPM, half-time feel
0---0-0---0--- | 0-0---0--3-2-- |
Kick follows chugs; snare lands huge on 3.
```

#### Doom/sludge slow crush

```text
Tempo: 68 BPM
| E5 | B♭5 | A5 | E5 |
Let chords decay. Do not fill every gap.
```

## Rhythm and drums

| Device | Effect |
|---|---|
| Fast straight 8ths | punk urgency |
| D-beat-like drive | hardcore/punk propulsion |
| Double-time drums over mid-tempo riff | creates panic without changing guitar tempo |
| Half-time breakdown | makes the same tempo feel heavier |
| Gallop rhythm | classic metal propulsion |
| Blast-like texture | extreme intensity; use only when the style wants it |
| Stop-time hits | crowd impact, mosh cue, lyric punctuation |
| Odd grouping over 4/4 | prog/djent tension |

A heavy section often works because the drums simplify at the right moment. If every instrument plays maximum density, the impact can shrink.

## Guitar and bass idioms

### Guitar

- Power chords are default because distortion makes full triads muddy.
- Palm muting controls weight more than the chord symbol does.
- Open low strings make riffs feel idiomatic; transposition can break the part.
- Down-tuning changes both range and hand feel.
- Octave shapes cut through without adding harmonic complexity.
- Tremolo picking creates sustain-through-motion.

### Bass

- Bass can double the guitar riff for weight, but strategic divergence makes sections bigger.
- In punk/hardcore, eighth-note root motion can be the engine.
- In metal, bass may reinforce low guitar, add fills between vocal phrases, or lock with kick accents.
- In a breakdown, simpler bass often hits harder.

See `../instrument-idiom/guitar.md`, `../instrument-idiom/bass.md`, and `../instrument-idiom/drums-percussion.md` for playability.

## Vocals and hooks

Heavy vocals are highly style-specific. Composition guidance should avoid assuming that every vocalist can scream, belt, growl, or sustain high notes safely.

Useful hook strategies:

- gang vocal on the title
- call-response between lead and band
- screamed verse + sung chorus contrast
- rhythmic chant over one pitch
- melodic chorus after riff-heavy verse
- short repeated phrase before breakdown

### Lyric tone

| Frame | Lyric tendencies |
|---|---|
| Punk | direct, social, sarcastic, alienated, funny, blunt |
| Hardcore | communal, confrontational, ethical, scene-centered, cathartic |
| Metal | mythic, dark, existential, technical, political, horror, fantasy |
| Pop-punk | youth, relationships, irony, self-deprecation, nostalgia |
| Emo/post-hardcore | confession, tension, rupture, memory, interior conflict |

Do not force violent or grim content. Heavy music can be joyous, absurd, spiritual, political, technical, or cathartic.

## Form

Common forms:

```text
Intro riff → Verse → Chorus → Verse → Chorus → Bridge / breakdown → Final chorus
```

```text
Riff A → Riff B → Verse → Riff A → Breakdown → Riff C → Outro
```

```text
Fast section → stop-time chant → half-time breakdown → double-time return
```

Heavy forms often use **section contrast** more than smooth transition. Abruptness can be a feature if it is cued by a drum fill, scream, pick scrape, or stop.

## Arrangement and production-facing composition

Composition choices that make production easier:

- leave space between low guitar, bass, and kick
- avoid stacking full six-note guitar chords under dense cymbals
- place pick attacks and kick transients deliberately
- double rhythm guitars only when the part is tight enough
- use octaves or single-note hooks to make choruses cut
- reserve cymbal wash for climaxes; too much wash hides riff detail
- make breakdowns rhythmically memorable, not merely low and slow

## Quick recipes

### Make a pop song punkier

- raise tempo or imply double-time
- simplify chords to power chords
- shorten verse lines
- add gang vocal after the hook
- make drums more direct; fewer ghost-note details

### Make a rock riff heavier

- move the riff to a lower open-string center
- palm-mute selected notes, not everything
- remove full triads from distorted guitars
- let bass and kick lock on the main accents
- put a rest before the biggest hit

### Make a breakdown hit

- simplify pitch material
- widen the register: low chugs + high noise/lead/scream
- switch to half-time snare placement
- add one memorable rhythmic cell
- stop everything for one beat before the return

## Common pitfalls

- **Do not write full keyboard chords and expect them to translate to distorted guitar.** Guitar shapes and distortion physics matter.
- **Do not confuse heaviness with constant density.** Rests and contrast make impact.
- **Do not flatten punk, hardcore, and metal into one angry sound.** Their histories, tempos, scenes, and ethics differ.
- **Do not overcomplicate punk unless the user asks for prog/post-hardcore.** Directness is a craft value.
- **Do not ignore tuning.** A riff written in E standard may lose its identity when moved to drop C, and vice versa.
- **Do not assume every vocalist can do extreme techniques.** Ask or keep alternatives.

## Quick decision matrix

| User wants | Try |
|---|---|
| More punk | faster tempo, downstrokes, short phrases, gang hook |
| More hardcore | stop-time, shouted rhythm, half-time impact, fewer chords |
| More metal | lower pedal riffs, modal minor, palm muting, guitar/bass/kick lock |
| More doom | slower tempo, longer decay, fewer notes, chromatic weight |
| More pop-punk | bright chorus, octave guitars, I-V-vi-IV, big backing vocals |
| More metalcore | syncopated chugs, screamed/sung contrast, breakdown architecture |
| More prog/djent | odd groupings, extended-range register, metric displacement |
